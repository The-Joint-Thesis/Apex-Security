Apex Security: A Predictive IoT Wearable System with Multi-Physics Hazard Modelling for Electrical Utility Worker Safety

Ratandeep Choudhary1
Shreyash Dwivedi2
CSVTU · Dept. of Electrical Engineering · UPU Govt. Polytechnic, Durg, C.G.
DOI 10.70849/ijsci
E-ISSN 3049-0251
Published 13 April 2026
Pages 1298–1309
License CC BY 4.0
Abstract

Traditional personal protective equipment in electrical utility environments remains fundamentally reactive, mitigating physical harm only after hazardous exposure occurs. This paper presents Apex Security, a next-generation IoT-enabled smart jacket that transitions industrial safety from passive protection to predictive prevention through real-time multi-physics hazard modeling, sensor fusion, and cumulative exposure dosimetry.

The system integrates seven sensing modalities within a flame-retardant garment: a high-impedance non-contact electric field sensor (ADA4530-1-based) with lock-in amplification for 50/60 Hz discrimination, MPU6050 inertial measurement unit for kinematic monitoring, MAX30102 photoplethysmography sensor for cardiac and SpO₂ measurement, and MLX90614 infrared thermometry for core body temperature estimation. All sensors interface with an ESP32-S3 microcontroller executing an Extended Kalman Filter for sensor fusion and a Random Forest classifier for hazard prediction.

Unlike existing wearables that merely report instantaneous measurements, Apex Security introduces three novel capabilities: (1) predictive hazard detection using machine learning to identify precursor patterns 5–10 seconds before incidents, (2) cumulative electric field dosimetry tracking long-term exposure against IEEE C95.1-2019 occupational limits, and (3) multi-channel communication (LoRaWAN + Wi-Fi) enabling reliable data transmission in metallic substation environments where conventional wireless fails. Laboratory validation demonstrates 96.8% fall detection sensitivity, 42 cm detection range for 11 kV conductors, and a 73% reduction in false alerts compared to commercial systems.
